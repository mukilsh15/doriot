inference infrastructure
The Inference Cost Squeeze Creates a $1B Tooling Market

Every AI product team is bleeding on inference costs, and the gap between what naive serving delivers and what's achievable with speculative decoding and async prefill is now 5-10x. The next infrastructure winner abstracts this complexity behind a simple API. This is the Datadog moment for LLM serving.

Why now: Speculative decoding only became production-stable in late 2025. Model sizes crossed a threshold where naive serving is economically unviable for any product at scale. The window for tooling is open now, before the hyperscalers absorb this layer.
Supporting signals: vllm-project/vllm: 10x throughput via speculative decoding (github), Anyscale raises $100M to scale Ray-based AI infrastructure (techcrunch)
MLOps
AI-Native Orchestration Displaces Airflow for the ML Stack

Airflow was built for ETL, not for workflows where tasks are probabilistic, latencies are variable, and retries require semantic awareness. The ML stack needs an orchestrator that treats LLM calls as first-class primitives — with built-in handling for rate limits, token budgets, and partial outputs.

Why now: The volume of LLM-in-production deployments hit a critical mass in 2025 that made the impedance mismatch with existing orchestration intolerable. Prefect 3.0 and similar launches signal that the market is ready to pay for a purpose-built replacement.
Supporting signals: Show HN: Prefect 3.0 — AI-native pipeline orchestration (hn), Why I switched from Airflow to DuckDB + dbt for our ML feature pipeline (reddit)
